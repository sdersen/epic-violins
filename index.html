Handmade violins of premium quality

May

  • Built: May, 2021
  • Luthier: Jacob Samarin
  • Wood: Maple, Spruce
  • Size: 4/4
  • Fine tuners: 1

May

This violin's slim body and wide arches, reminiscent of a Guarneri, gives it a round and distinct timbre, while not removing the warm and grounded tone.

It is equipped with Dominant strings, producing a sound close to gut strings.

Our archetier recommends a wooden bow, to match the smooth and warm sound of the instrument.

Do you want to try playing May?

June

  • Built: June, 2021
  • Luthier: Esmeralda Llorca
  • Wood: Maple, Spruce
  • Size: 4/4
  • Fine tuners: 1

June

This violin has a distinct sound with a firm and grounded tone. Its Amati-like shape with smaller bottom arches brings even more precision to the sound.

It is equipped with steel core strings, bringing out the brilliance of the overtones.

Our archetier recommends a carbon fiber bow, to enhance precision and speed.

Do you want to try playing June?

July

  • Built: July, 2021
  • Luthier: Kim Breitkopf
  • Wood: Maple, Spruce
  • Size: 3/4
  • Fine tuners: 2

July

This three-quarter violin is perfect for younger, ambitious violinists. It is inspired by Steiner's violins and has a round and warm sound while also producing a bright and distinct timbre.

It is equipped with steel core strings, bringing out the brilliance of the overtones.

Both carbon fiber and wooden bows work well with this violin.

Voices about Epic Violins

Sarah Chang

Ray Chen

"Like wind can carve stone, the vibration can carve wood over a few hundred years. That’s why I think certain instruments sounds the way they do. When you have a modern violin, like my Epic Violin, it’s open, and it hasn’t been set yet, so you get to go in and you have to set it yourself — every note."

Arabella Steinbacher

“I’ve played this violin for quite a while now and it's a wonderful instrument. It’s not only brilliant from the sound perspective; it’s very big and bright, yet it also has a lot of warmth and darkness. Mostly though, it’s interesting to see how it has changed over the years, just as I have.”
